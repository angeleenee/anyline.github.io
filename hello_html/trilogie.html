La Trilogie Coréene by Ook Chung

An enthralling book exploring the author's diasporic family history Even though it had a generally placid autobiographical tone, this novel was never predictable. I couldn't stop reading. I don't think I'm the only person who finds ancestral history fascinating, especially in a culture that is your own. In this case, the author was recounting stories from his exiled Korean family, who traveled to Japan and Canada. Additionally, the French writing was so poetic. The author explores the relation of Korea and poetry/literature, of it perhaps being a way for it to maintain its identity after undergoing so much separation.
