Adventures in Quebec city

This Easter weekend, I had the pleasure of going on a trip to Quebec city with my dad. Perhaps one of the highlights was spending time and practicing our French together, since we always converse in English.

One of the first places we visited was the Musée national des Beaux-Arts. We saw the exhibitions, a creative one about illusions, and the galleries with art from QUebec throughout the centuries. A most notable thing for me was the magnitude of sculptures made by Iroquois people. They were different, and reflected their culture.

Afterwards, we visited the charming Plaines d'Abraham and Old Quebec. It has a vibe that is distinct to that area. It always reminds me of the heart-warming K-drama Goblin when we walk there. Finally, we also got some ramen at Tori-ya. It was my second time having ramen in my lifetime, and the first time I went to such and authentic Japanese restaurant- it was packed! We were greeted with the Japanese expression for when you enter a restaurant, something I had never experienced before. It was a fun street with many cool restaurants, I'd definitely like to go again. Read more
